Travel Information

Cyprus is served by two international airports: Larnaca International Airport (LCA) and Paphos International Airport (PFO). More than 50 international airlines connect Cyprus to the world. Visit the official website for details: www.hermesairports.com.



Airport Transfers

Conference participants have several transfer options:

  • Private Taxi Transfers: Transfers can be booked online through EasyConferences. Services include private or shared taxis with meet-and-assist at the airport. Trusted collaborators provide the service, supervised by EasyConferences.
  • Airport Taxis: Taxis are available on arrival at both airports. Service is reliable but may involve some waiting, and fares may vary depending on the time.
  • Public Transportation: Cyprus offers an extensive public bus network, connecting major cities and airports. Visit Cyprus by Bus for more details.


From Larnaca Airport to Limassol

Step 1: Use the Limassol Airport Express shuttle service (50 minutes) to reach Limassol. The last bus stop is “Saint George Havouzas.” Check the timetable at www.limassolairportexpress.eu.
Step 2: From the bus stop, take a taxi or public buses (EMEL) to your hotel. Visit www.limassolbuses.com for bus routes and timetables.


From Paphos Airport to Limassol

Option 1: Take the Limassol Airport Express shuttle service (45 minutes) to Limassol. Timetables are available at www.limassolairportexpress.eu. From the bus stop, use local buses (EMEL) or taxis to your hotel.

Option 2: Use public bus route 613 from Paphos Airport to Pervola-Karavela Station. Then, take the Intercity Buses to Limassol (New Port Station). Visit Intercity Buses for routes. From New Port Station, take an EMEL bus or taxi to your hotel. For EMEL details, visit www.limassolbuses.com.


Local Transportation in Limassol

Bus 30: This bus connects St. Raphael Resort with the city center along the Limassol seafront avenue. Buses run from Monday to Sunday, from 06:00 until after midnight, every 15 minutes. Day tickets cost €1.50, night tickets (after 21:00) cost €2.50, and daily unlimited tickets cost €5.

Touristic Information

Limassol, known locally as "Lemessos," is the second-largest city in Cyprus and a thriving hub of culture, history, and tourism. Located between the ancient kingdoms of Amathus and Kourion, Limassol is renowned for its beautiful coastline, historic landmarks, and vibrant nightlife. The city offers a perfect blend of modern metropolitan living and ancient charm.



Top Attractions in Limassol

Limassol Castle: This iconic castle, where Richard the Lionheart is believed to have married Princess Berengaria of Navarre, now serves as a medieval museum. Explore its extensive collection of artifacts from the 400-1870 AD period.

Kolossi Medieval Castle: Located 11 km west of Limassol, this 15th-century castle was once home to the Knights of St. John of Jerusalem. It is a must-see for history enthusiasts.

Amathus Ruins: One of the oldest imperial cities in Cyprus, featuring ancient temples, statues, and breathtaking views of the Mediterranean. It's a key site in Greek mythology, closely associated with Aphrodite.

Kourion Archaeological Site: A stunning Greco-Roman archaeological site with a well-preserved theater, mosaics, and an early Christian basilica. Located 18 km west of Limassol.

Municipal Garden: Situated along the coastal road, this garden provides a tranquil escape with pathways, a zoo, and an open-air garden theater hosting events throughout the year.

Limassol Marina: A modern waterfront development featuring luxury residences, a full-service marina, and a vibrant shopping and dining area.


Touring Cyprus

Cyprus is a Mediterranean gem, offering diverse experiences across its cities and countryside. From the historical sites of Paphos and Nicosia to the picturesque Troodos Mountains and pristine beaches, there’s something for every traveler. Don't miss:

  • Aphrodite's Rock: The legendary birthplace of the goddess of love and beauty.
  • Troodos Mountains: Ideal for hiking, skiing, and exploring Byzantine monasteries.
  • Kyrenia: Famous for its harbor and Kyrenia Castle, a scenic town in Northern Cyprus.
